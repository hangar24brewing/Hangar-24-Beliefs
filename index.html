The Heart of Who We Are

At Hangar 24, a great business isn’t just about what we make—it’s about who we are, how we work and how we build each other up. Our core beliefs aren’t abstract ideals hanging on a wall; they are living principles that flow through every batch we brew, every plate we serve and every conversation we share. They shape our decisions when no one is watching and remind us who we truly are in the moments that define us.

Time‑Honored American Values

Strong individuals build strong communities. Hangar 24 draws on a heritage of character that crossed oceans with pilgrims, cleared wilderness with pioneers and built a nation from an impossible dream. These seven foundational values create the bedrock upon which everything else is built.

TruthfulReality doesn’t negotiate—it simply is.

Living truthfully means having the courage to pursue what’s real, speak what’s accurate and welcome what’s hard—because you genuinely care. We choose truth even when it costs convenience, trusting that transparency builds the only foundation strong enough to bear real weight.

  • Report issues immediately rather than hiding them.
  • Admit when you don’t know something; seek accurate information.
  • Be transparent about product limitations and timing.
LovingLove sees what is and chooses what could be.

Love is commitment to another’s good even when there’s nothing in it for you. It doesn’t wait for worthiness; it creates it. When convenience conflicts with care, love chooses care. When personal gain conflicts with another’s good, love chooses good.

  • Invest personal time mentoring and supporting colleagues.
  • Advocate for customers and guests, even at your own cost.
  • Accommodate unique needs as opportunities to serve.
Servant‑HeartedStrength finds its highest expression in service.

True power reveals itself through restraint. Those who can lead choose to lift; those who could demand decide to give. Service is not subservience—it’s strength directed toward elevating others.

  • Leaders handle tedious tasks alongside their teams.
  • Take difficult assignments to protect less experienced staff.
  • Share opportunities generously so everyone grows.
GoodGoodness sees need and simply responds.

Goodness is the natural tendency to see what helps, heals and builds up—and then act on it. It doesn’t calculate kindness or keep score; it intervenes when something is broken and refuses to participate in harm.

  • Step in when someone is struggling or equipment is stressed.
  • Protect colleagues from unreasonable demands or customers.
  • Offer honest advice even when it may reduce immediate sales.
Faithful StewardsFaithfulness is the compound interest of character.

Small promises kept create large trust. We honor what we’ve been entrusted with—our bodies, minds, relationships and work—through thousands of small decisions that build reliability. Faithfulness turns words into bonds when tested by time and circumstance.

  • Maintain standards even when under pressure or unobserved.
  • Follow up on promises consistently and thoroughly.
  • Care for resources with the same attention in every situation.
HumbleHumility builds bridges where pride builds walls.

Humility isn’t thinking less of yourself; it’s thinking of yourself less. It’s the wisdom to know what you don’t know and the strength to admit it. Humility keeps us teachable, opens doors to deeper understanding and allows us to learn from anyone.

  • Ask questions of newcomers and value fresh perspectives.
  • Publicly acknowledge mistakes and adjust based on feedback.
  • Give credit to others and celebrate team contributions.
JoyfulJoy is contagious rebellion against despair.

Joy isn’t the absence of difficulty but the presence of perspective. It finds meaning in carrying the weight and shares hope when situations feel heavy. Celebration and laughter become acts of defiance against cynicism.

  • Find satisfaction in the daily rhythms of transformation.
  • Bring warmth to interactions even during hard shifts.
  • Approach challenges with optimism and belief in solutions.

Why It Matters

These seven qualities weave together like strands in a rope—truth without love becomes harsh, love without goodness becomes enabling, and goodness without humility becomes self‑righteousness. Character holds when everything else gives way. We hire for it, nurture it and let it become the bedrock on which we build our craft and community.

Masterful Craftsmanship

Throughout history, the greatest craftsmen have understood that their work connects them to forces larger than themselves. Excellence emerges when skill becomes stewardship and craft becomes calling. These seven principles reveal why some work transcends function to become art and why craftsmanship is more than making things well—it’s creating things that matter.

Partnership with PurposeWe are designed to create, built to build.

The finest craftsmen recognize that their abilities are gifts and that creativity flows from sources beyond personal effort. This understanding transforms arrogance into gratitude and performance into stewardship.

  • Approach processes with reverence for traditions and natural laws.
  • See talent as a resource to be cultivated, not owned.
  • Remain grateful for the opportunity to participate in meaningful work.
Reverence for DetailExcellence lives in the margins others overlook.

Invisible foundations determine visible beauty. True craftsmen invest care in details that matter precisely because others might ignore them. They measure twice, check constantly and refuse shortcuts that compromise quality for convenience.

  • Follow protocols faithfully when unobserved.
  • Maintain equipment beyond immediate necessity.
  • Obsess over the small things that elevate the whole.
Sacred StewardshipEverything entrusted to us demands faithful care.

Resources, time, opportunities and relationships are temporary trusts. Treat them with conscious care—barley represents months of labor, a guest’s evening is irreplaceable and a teammate’s growth cannot be regained once lost.

  • Use ingredients and materials with awareness of their true cost.
  • Manage time as the non‑renewable resource it is.
  • Treat tools and relationships as responsibilities, not commodities.
Work as ExpressionWhat we make reveals who we are.

Work isn’t separate from identity; it expresses it. Every brew, dish or service interaction reflects the character of its creator. Quality becomes a personal signature, while mediocrity becomes self‑betrayal.

  • Let consistency reflect personal reliability.
  • Treat each interaction as an opportunity to convey values.
  • Present work in a way that honours those who receive it.
Humble LearningMastery begins when we admit what we don’t know.

Pride builds walls around current knowledge; humility opens doors to deeper understanding. True masters remain teachable and seek instruction from mentors, mistakes and even those they teach.

  • Study new techniques regardless of seniority.
  • Learn from newcomers who see what familiarity misses.
  • Ask questions that reveal gaps rather than hiding them.
Patient ProcessExcellence cannot be rushed; it can only be reached.

Time and process aren’t negotiable. Fermentation, development and mastery follow natural rhythms. Excellence emerges from respecting those rhythms and resisting the temptation to hurry what requires patience.

  • Honour production schedules regardless of pressure.
  • Allow team members time to develop competence.
  • Build reputation through consistency over years rather than shortcuts.
Purposeful ServiceThe highest skill serves the deepest need.

Ultimate mastery reveals itself not in personal glory but in others’ flourishing. Skill without service becomes display; service without skill becomes well‑intentioned mediocrity. True excellence creates value that extends beyond yourself.

  • Develop systems that enable team success rather than showcasing yourself.
  • Mentor others so greatness multiplies through generosity.
  • Create work that strengthens community while expressing personal excellence.

Why It Matters

When these principles guide our craft, every decision carries deeper meaning and every perfectly prepared product becomes an expression of values that outlast a single meal or pour. Craftsmanship built on purpose doesn’t just create better beer, food and service—it creates better people, stronger community and work that matters.

Transformational Community

Even the finest ingredients require the right environment to reach their potential. At Hangar 24 we believe that true transformation happens in relationship. Our community isn’t just a group of people in the same place; it’s a culture built on encouragement, accountability and shared experiences. These five principles show how individual strength serves collective flourishing and how ordinary people become remarkable together.

Foundation PrinciplesCharacter & individuality

Transformational community begins with individuals of character who create space for others to flourish. Love sees worth beyond usefulness; honor recognizes inherent value regardless of position; acceptance embraces difference within shared values; kindness chooses consideration over convenience.

  • Love One Another: See each person’s value and seek their good.
  • Honor One Another: Treat every role with genuine respect.
  • Accept One Another: Embrace diverse perspectives within a shared mission.
  • Be Kind to One Another: Choose thoughtful action over indifference.
Communication PrinciplesHow we speak

Words create worlds. We commit to speech that builds trust and refuses to erode it. Truth honors others with reality rather than comfortable illusion. Encouragement highlights potential amid struggle. Instruction shares knowledge freely. Warnings speak up when danger looms. Honor in absence refuses gossip and invites direct conversation.

  • Speak Truth: Offer reality with courage and honesty.
  • Encourage: Highlight strengths and fuel determination.
  • Instruct: Share knowledge freely; teaching multiplies mastery.
  • Warn: Offer timely cautions when danger approaches.
  • Speak Honorably: Avoid gossip; protect reputations in absence.
Growth PrinciplesDevelopment & excellence

Progress requires both affirmation and correction. We admonish with respect, striving for excellence together. We seek wisdom collectively, turning complaint into contribution. We hold one another accountable with clear expectations and appropriate consequences. And we remain patient, recognizing that growth follows natural progression.

  • Admonish: Confront patterns that limit potential with care.
  • Strive for Excellence: Challenge each other toward mastery.
  • Seek Wisdom Together: Pursue deeper understanding collectively.
  • Hold Accountable: Establish expectations and address destructive behaviours.
  • Be Patient: Allow development to unfold without undue pressure.
Reconciliation PrinciplesHealing & harmony

Conflict is inevitable; division is a choice. These principles provide pathways back to unity. We bear with one another’s unfinished edges, forgive past wrongs, pursue peace by going directly to the source, and work in harmony by coordinating diverse strengths toward common goals.

  • Bear With One Another: Carry tolerance for quirks and weaknesses.
  • Forgive One Another: Release past wrongs to free both parties.
  • Pursue Peace: Address conflicts directly and seek unity.
  • Work in Harmony: Orchestrate differences into shared achievement.
Support PrinciplesCarrying & celebrating

True belonging emerges in how we handle vulnerability, need and success. The strongest among us lift others’ burdens, submit to expertise beyond our own, share loads when someone is overwhelmed and stand with each other in hardship. We celebrate achievements deliberately and create welcome that forges lasting belonging.

  • Serve One Another: Lift others’ burdens without fanfare.
  • Submit to One Another: Recognize when others’ expertise should lead.
  • Carry Burdens: Share weight when someone’s load exceeds capacity.
  • Stand With One Another: Offer presence without rushing to fix.
  • Celebrate With One Another: Mark achievements to reinforce values.
  • Create Welcome: Foster environments where everyone belongs.

Why It Matters

We are meant to live, work and grow together. A culture of strong, committed individuals combined with a relentless pursuit of excellence creates an unshakable community. When these principles govern our interactions, ordinary people become remarkable and collective transformation unfolds.

The Complete Brew

Character forms the foundation → Craft builds upon it → Community completes the transformation. Together they create something extraordinary: a brewery where the ancient gifts of beer, bread and celebration bring people together around a table and the abundant life transforms everything. We invite you to embody these beliefs in every task, interaction and decision.
